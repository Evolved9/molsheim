Bugatti × Emir Kolburan — 2025

MOLSHEIM

A conceptual football boot derived from the aerodynamic surface language of the Type 57 SC Atlantic — where racing heritage meets athletic precision.

Type 57 SC Atlantic Concept  /  #Molsheim Scroll
Design Brief

Speed,
Translated. From Alsace to the pitch.

The Molsheim project takes its name from Bugatti's spiritual home in Alsace, France — the town where Ettore Bugatti first forged the union of art and mechanical excellence. This boot is not a collaboration proposal; it is a design inquiry.

What happens when the sinuous dorsal spine of the Atlantic, its riveted aluminium flanks, and its aerodynamic arch are reinterpreted not in steel and carbon — but in textile, TPU, and turf-ready outsole geometry?

The answer is a form that belongs to both worlds: fast enough for the pitch, refined enough for the showroom.

Process Documentation
C-Line Form Exploration
01
Form Language

The C-line, derived from Bugatti's iconic radiator spine, was studied across twelve geometric variations — fluid, geometric, branded, and carbon-inflected.

02
Material Study

CMF exploration centred on the outsole: high-gloss transparent TPU allowing cleat geometry and light refraction to become visible structural elements.

03
Heritage Coding

The EB monogram and Type 57 SC lettering are integrated as typographic details — not applied branding, but surface-embedded identity marks.
